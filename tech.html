The Architecture

Conversation is no longer
a script.

Lelit Distrikt 2 is powered by a custom-built AI bridge inside Unreal Engine 5.7. It is 100% local, 100% private, and runs without latency. This is the end of the "Hello Traveller" era.

01

Gemma 4 Private Brain

Auggie thinks using high-reasoning local LLMs. No cloud, no lag, no privacy concerns. He remembers you, and every interaction is distilled into a persistent SQLite memory.

02

Identity-Driven Dialogue

NPCs and interactive objects possess unique conversation identities, leading to highly personalized, context-aware interactions that evolve as they remember past encounters.

03

Player-Owned Runtime

A robust C++ architecture ensures a stable conversation lifecycle. Enter text chat seamlessly and return to game exploration without losing control, driven entirely by localized logic.

04

Adaptive Voice Communication

While a robust text chat acts as the universal baseline, users with capable hardware can engage in full natural voice-to-voice conversations powered by integrated TTS & STT systems.

05

Out-of-Bound Messaging

Auggie's presence isn't strictly confined to the game world. In the final release, he can proactively reach out and message the player directly through WhatsApp.

Exploration Phase

2025Identity

World Direction

Establishing the definitive setting and aesthetic.

  • Mediterranean town setting selected
  • Cohesive environment identity established
  • Refined artistic direction for the district
Early 2026Now

AI Core (Current State)

Foundation of the autonomous interaction system.

  • Local AI conversation: Powered by local LM Studio inference; stable text chat path with auto-discovery.
  • Player-owned runtime: UPlayerConversationComponent owns request flow, widget state, and routing (C++).
  • Identity-driven targets: Talkable NPCs and objects use UConversationIdentityComponent.
  • Persistent memory: SQLite-based storage in ConversationMemory.db.
  • Stable lifecycle: Seamless enter, converse, and return to game-only input.
  • Technical baseline: UE 5.7; high-end PC target with Nanite/Lumen.
