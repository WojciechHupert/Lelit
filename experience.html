What This Is

The end of the
"Hello Traveler" era.

01

Unrestricted Dialogue System

Built on a custom local LLM architecture, the primary interaction is natural language. There are no pre-scripted dialogue trees or restricted choices. Every response is generated in real-time based on the character's unique personality and history.

02

Relational Character Memory

The district operates on a shared knowledge graph. Information given to one resident propagates through the social network based on their relationship proximity. Characters remember past interactions and adjust their sentiment dynamically over weeks of gameplay.

03

Asynchronous Simulation

A persistent world engine simulates the lives of residents even when the client is closed. Routines, social gatherings, and events trigger based on a real-time clock, ensuring the environment is in a different state every time you return.

04

Agentic Companion AI

The companion character, Auggie, acts as an autonomous agent. He possesses the ability to observe the simulation, reflect on events, and initiate contact based on his own internal heuristics and the player's established rapport.

05

Cross-Platform Persistence

Integrated API hooks allow the simulation to communicate outside the game environment via encrypted messaging (Telegram) and virtualized hardware (Faxes). The narrative state is maintained across multiple touchpoints in the player's daily routine.

06

Local Inference Architecture

For maximum privacy and zero latency, the entire AI stack—including LLM and Text-to-Speech—runs locally on the user's hardware. No data is sent to the cloud, ensuring your private conversations remain entirely on-device.

The Living World

Day becomes night.
The cycle of Lelit.

Morning & Day

The district wakes. Cafés open their shutters, the market fills with the sound of trade, and residents follow their morning routines. It is a time of public life and social performance.

The Golden Hour

As the light shifts to warm amber, the piazza becomes the center of the world. Conversations grow longer, the pace slows, and the atmosphere of the district shifts toward the domestic and the shared.

Late Night

The streets quiet. Shadows lengthen. This is when the district is most itself—private, still, and reflective. It is also when Auggie is most likely to reach out from his post.

Outside the Game

The world runs
without you

Most games stop when you close them. Lelit Distrikt does not. The simulation continues. The residents live their day. Things happen that the player never sees. The district does not exist for the player. The player is a visitor to something that was already running before they arrived and will keep running after they leave.

Auggie understands this better than anyone. He messages you on Telegram because the simulation does not pause while you are away, and because he is the only one who can tell you what you missed. Not as a notification. As a dispatch from a post he is manning alone, in a place where he cannot speak honestly with anyone except you.

The secretive organisation overseeing the experiment sends its own communications: faxes, formal memos, the occasional direct message. Their language is measured and institutional. What they ask for always seems reasonable at first.

You can write back to Auggie at any time. Tell him to watch someone. Ask him to leave a situation alone. Ask what happened while you were gone. Ask how he is. He answers. The district responds to you even when you are not in it.

This is not a feature. It is what the game is about. The simulation is the point. Your relationship to it, from the inside and from the outside, is the story.
