Section 1

What Lelit Distrikt
is about

Lelit Distrikt is a sealed world—a social experiment preserved beneath a dome. The outside world collapsed, but they were never told.

The project is an exploration of people, observation, and the weight of memory. It is a precise, intentional construction that creates a specific emotional experience. You aren't here to solve a puzzle or win a war; you are here to witness the texture of human life that was almost lost.

The town looks real, the characters feel real, and the sky above is a projection of a normality that no longer exists.

Section 2

Why this world
exists

The Enclosure was built to preserve a record of how we lived before—our relationships, routines, conflicts, and small daily kindnesses. It is simultaneously an archive, an experiment, and a memorial.

The dome protects more than just people; it protects an "archive quality" of life. We are preserving ways of living and routines rather than just plot events. It is a transition from a world that moved on to something post-human, back to something embodied, local, and finite.

Section 3

The player’s role

The Observer

You stand on the outside of their reality, watching the patterns of a society that believes its horizon is real. Your gaze is not neutral; to observe is to curate.

The Participant

By engaging with the residents, you become part of their world. You form genuine connections across a moral asymmetry that only you and Auggie understand.

The Witness

You are an anthropologist of the ordinary. You gather stories, notice the unsaid, and hold the weight of truths that the residents cannot yet bear.

The Interpreter

You decide what to report to Management and what to keep as a secret. You define the value of these simulated lives through your own attention.

Section 4

The district
as a stage

The town of Lelit is more than a backdrop; it is a protagonist. The central square is the informal social infrastructure where everyone passes through and every story converges.

Inspired by the ensemble model of great television, the district has its own rhythm—cafés that open with the sun and a piazza that fills with the evening light. It is a Mediterranean social space where the atmosphere itself tells a story of a world frozen in a perfect, messy normality.

Section 5

Story and relationships

Narrative in Lelit Distrikt is not a checklist of tasks; it is a web of hidden lives and unresolved tensions that you uncover over time.

Hidden Lives

Every resident has a secret, a philosophy, and a history that predates your arrival. Their lives continue even when you aren't watching.

Character Webs

Information moves like gossip. What you say to one person might be reflected back to you by another, days later, in a completely different context.

Unresolved Tensions

You step into the middle of long-simmering conflicts and quiet loves. You don't "fix" them; you navigate them, and your presence adds weight to the outcome.

Returning to People

The most important moments aren't plot beats—they are conversations about small things, at the right time, with someone who remembers you were there.

Section 6

The project’s creative intention

Lelit Distrikt is a slower, more reflective kind of experience. The engine is dialogue, not physics. We believe that the act of paying full attention to ordinary people is radical and meaningful.

Our intention is to create an emotional experience that lingers after you close the game—a question about what makes a life "real" and whether a perfect replica of a world is any less significant than the original.
